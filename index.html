Verified Licensing Information (2025)

  • CC BY 4.0: Creative Commons Attribution 4.0 International - current standard for most jurisdictions
  • CC BY 4.0 AU: Creative Commons Attribution 4.0 Australia - South Australia's preferred license
  • CC BY 3.0 AU: Creative Commons Attribution 3.0 Australia - legacy licensing (Tasmania)
  • Commercial: Requires paid licensing agreements (cadastre, premium services)
  • Restricted: Special permissions required (Sacred Sites, VAR licenses)
  • Open Data: Available without Creative Commons framework but open access
  • Federal (BoM): Bureau of Meteorology federal licensing for Geofabric data

Service Types Identified

  • ArcGIS MapServer: Standard ArcGIS REST services for map visualization and querying
  • ArcGIS REST: ArcGIS REST services with full feature access
  • WFS (Web Feature Service): OGC standard for vector data access and download
  • WMS (Web Map Service): OGC standard for map image services
  • Download (SHP): Direct shapefile downloads available
  • Commercial Access: Requires payment or special licensing arrangements
  • Service type: Unknown: Available but service type not yet determined

Key Licensing Insights from 2025 Research

  • Standard Migration: Most jurisdictions have moved to CC BY 4.0 International from earlier CC BY 3.0 AU versions
  • Cadastre Restrictions: NT and SA require commercial arrangements for current cadastral data; Victoria has dual open/commercial model
  • Attribution Requirements: All CC licenses require attribution to respective state governments with specific format requirements
  • Commercial Use Rights: Creative Commons licenses uniformly permit commercial use across all jurisdictions
  • Tasmania Transition: Still using legacy CC BY 3.0 AU for many spatial datasets through TheList portal
  • Premium Services: Victoria operates sophisticated dual system with free basic data and commercial premium services

Notable Access Restrictions

  • Sacred Sites (NT): Cultural restrictions requiring Aboriginal Areas Protection Authority consultation
  • SA Cadastre: Value Added Reseller (VAR) licence required through Land Services SA
  • NT Current Data: ILIS/NTLIS systems require 2-4 week licensing process for current property/planning data
  • Victoria Premium: High-resolution imagery, elevation data, and detailed cadastre require annual subscriptions
  • Federal Data: Geofabric managed by Bureau of Meteorology under separate federal licensing framework

Implementation Recommendations

  • Attribution Compliance: Ensure attribution meets specific state requirements - formats vary significantly
  • License Verification: Check individual dataset metadata as portal-wide policies may have exceptions
  • Commercial Planning: Budget for cadastral data access in NT, SA, and premium Victoria services
  • Legacy Support: Plan for ongoing support of CC BY 3.0 AU data from Tasmania until migration occurs
  • Cultural Sensitivity: Implement appropriate consultation processes for heritage and sacred site data
